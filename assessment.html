Assessments


These include tests of intelligence and achievement for students with low/no vision.

Tactile Digit Symbol Coding - Processing Speed

This test was created with Amanda McKerracher for measuring processing speed in individuals with visual impairments. Results on the validity and reliability of this test will be released shortly, and were the main topic of Amanda's PhD dissertation.

The top of the test shows 5 symbols, labeled, from left to right, 1-5 in braille. The task is to name the number that goes with each symbol for the lower 6 rows of symbols, as quickly as possible. The first, partial row is a practice row to ensure the participant understands the task. In total, the test takes roughly 5 minutes to administer.

The tactile digit symbol coding test was created by laser cutting a cardboard background, creating holes so that air could pass through during thermoforming and etching the locations of every symbol. The symbols were laser cut from poster board (0.06 inch elevation, 0.025 inch line widths), and glued onto the background over the etching using wood glue. This form was then placed in a thermoform machine to make durable copies of the test.
