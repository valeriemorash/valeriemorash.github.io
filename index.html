About

Val Morash is a postdoctoral research fellow at the Smith-Kettlewell Eye Research Institute in San Francisco. She has undergraduate degrees in Electrical Engineering and Brain and Cognitive Sciences from MIT, a Master's in Electrical Engineering from MIT, a Master's in Statistics from the University of California, Berkeley, and a PhD in Psychology from the University of California, Berkeley.

Val's research is focused on haptic perception, in individuals with and without vision. She also studies education and accessibility for people with visual impairments. Her underlying interest is in how human cognition and knowledge is shaped by the senses.

Val grew up in Michigan, near Michigan State University, where both of her parents are professors, Dr. Merry (Criminology) and emeritus Dr. Ed Morash (Business). Her greatest adolescent rebellion against her social-scientist parents involved attending MIT, majoring in engineering, and preferentially selecting courses with the word “computational” in the title.

She enjoys playing the violin, gardening, and paddle boarding. She has a large collection of hand and power tools, as well as a laser cutter. Val is most passionate about traveling, and recent trips include India (2012), Portugal/Spain (2012), Tanzania (2013), Vietnam (2014), Sweden/Denmark (2015), and Lisbon/Prague (2015).
